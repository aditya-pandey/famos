Our Origins

It started with a simple question: Are we really connected?

famos wasn't born in a boardroom. It was born at a dinner table where three generations sat together, yet felt worlds apart. We realized that while technology connects the world, it often creates invisible walls within our own homes.

Julian Aris

Founder & Father of Three

Parenting in the Age of Noise

Today’s families are navigating an unprecedented landscape. Between the constant pull of social media, the rising pressures of academic performance, and the sheer speed of digital life, the space for quiet, meaningful connection is shrinking.

We saw parents struggling to find the "manual" for modern relating. We saw teens who wanted to share their worlds but didn't know how to bridge the gap. The tools we use for work—Slack, Notion, Trello—are designed for high-performance productivity. Why weren't there tools designed for high-performance empathy?

"We don't need more tracking; we need more understanding. We don't need more control; we need more agency."

famos was built on the belief that a family is an ecosystem, not a hierarchy. By providing a structured, gentle environment for communication, task-sharing, and emotional check-ins, we can lower the cognitive load of "running a family" and make room for the joy of "being a family."

Our platform uses behavioral science and emotional intelligence to nudge families toward better habits. It's not about monitoring; it's about making the invisible emotional labor of home life visible, valued, and shared.
